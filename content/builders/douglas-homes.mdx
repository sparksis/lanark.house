My experience with Douglas Homes was my first attempt at purchasing a new home. The property was at **204 Langwell Common SE**, **Phase 4, Block 13, Lot 1** of **Lanark Landing**, a community developed by **Melcor** in Airdrie, Alberta.

My salesperson was **Trish Braun**. I signed the contract on November 12, 2024 and put down a **$5,000 bank draft** as my initial deposit.

From the outset, there was a refusal to provide a sample contract for review before signing. After signing, Trish stated that Schedule C (Home Warranty) was not attached and that I wasn't entitled to view it until closing. According to Colton's recorded account (R2-Q4, Nov 20, 2024 recording), his legal name was wrong on the contract — "That signature is with Colton English, the contract's with Colton Wade" — and Trish reportedly suggested he sign with the wrong name rather than reissuing the contract. {/* R2-Q4: DocuSign recording, Nov 20 2024 */} The DocuSign documents came from a personal email address, e-signed by "DW" through a numbered corporation — not Douglas Homes' official system. Colton noted in a same-day recording that the DocuSign invitation "goes to a random numbered Corporation," and office staff confirmed they could not explain this routing. {/* R2-Q1: DocuSign recording, Nov 20 2024 */}

The deal-breaking issue was an 8-inch discrepancy between the as-built home and the minimum building width specified under **Section 3.2.2** of the Lanark Landing architectural guidelines. I asked Douglas Homes to obtain a formal waiver from **E2 and Associates** (the architectural committee) under **Section 10** of the guidelines. They refused.

My Schedule A specified a **[Fiberglass Hytec AC759-O](https://www.hytec.ca/product-detail/AC759?skuid=AC759-0)** bathtub, whose near-full-height dimensions were a critical requirement.

The deal collapsed on November 20, 2024. My deposit was refunded.
