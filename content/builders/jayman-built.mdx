My second attempt at purchasing a new home was with Jayman Built. The property was at **71 Legacy Reach Court SE** in the **Legacy** community, Calgary. The model was an **Avid 20 with revised ensuite**. My sales associate was **Brittney Mack**.

I received the purchase agreement on **March 4, 2025**. Key line items: **$7,820** for the "Revised Ensuite" (4-piece ensuite with separate soaker tub and shower) and **$12,600** for "Fit and Finish Extra (2)."

The core dispute was over the ensuite shower. Jayman's [Fit and Finish Brochure](https://www.jayman.com/assets/Uploads/JAY-2609-Fit-and-Finish-Brochure-CAL_WEB_2025.pdf) described: *"4-piece ensuites include curbless tiled shower recessed into the floor sloped towards a 32" linear drain. Walls are tiled to ceiling. Includes 80" height, chrome frame, 10 mil glass shower door."* The contract was vague, referencing only "other shower stalls applicable to plan."

Post-signing, **Brittney Mack** initially stated the spec would be 70"/5mil glass (April 10), revised to 75"/6mil (April 11) — neither matching the brochure. Jayman quoted nearly $7,000 to "upgrade" to the advertised specs. Escalation to sales manager **Michael Klassen** was requested on April 11 but met with 16 days of silence.

On **April 28, 2025**, I sent a formal dispute to **Stephanie Myers** (VP, Single Family), CC'd to **David Hooge** (COO) and **Jay Westman** (Chairman & CEO), citing *contra proferentem* and requesting the brochure specs plus written confirmation of the **Navien NPE 240A tankless water heater**.

Jayman Built terminated the agreement in early May 2025.
