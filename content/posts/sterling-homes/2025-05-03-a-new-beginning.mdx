After two failed attempts at buying a new home — Douglas Homes in November 2024 and Jayman Built from March to May 2025 — I was running low on optimism. Then I found Sterling Homes, and the difference was immediate.

**The first meeting.**

My first point of contact was **Jason Tang** (lanark@sterlingcalgary.com), and there was no sales pressure, no vague hand-waving — just straight answers. After my experience with a salesperson who sent contracts from a personal email and a builder whose brochure specs didn't match the contract, Jason's directness was a relief.

The home was a **quick-possession "Luna" model** at **728 Langley Terrace SE, Airdrie, AB** (job number J#158004). A quick-possession home meant I could see exactly what I was buying — no more speculation about what "applicable to plan" might mean.

**The contract.**

The purchase agreement (`158004_-_Sterling_Homes_Purchase_Agreementpd.pdf`) was comprehensive and clear. Every schedule was present, every specification was detailed, and there were no glaring omissions or ambiguous clauses. The home warranty — a document I had to fight to even see with Douglas Homes — was included as a matter of course.

**Why it mattered.**

The home at 728 Langley Terrace was more affordable than the property I had considered in Legacy with Jayman. But the benefits didn't stop at the price tag. I was able to get the features that were most important to me, including a full-height tile bathroom — a detail that had been the centre of my dispute with Jayman — and a front garage.

The quick-possession timeline was a major advantage. I would be settled into my new house before Jayman had even broken ground on the Legacy lot, and long before the Douglas Homes property at 204 Langwell Common SE would have been completed.

**Not perfect, but honest.**

I don't want to paint Sterling Homes as flawless. The post-possession experience would bring its own challenges — a site supervisor whose interpersonal skills didn't match his construction knowledge, a warranty app with critical bugs, and repairs that tested my patience. I'll cover all of that in later posts.

But the sales process — the part that had gone so badly wrong twice before — was transparent, efficient, and respectful. After months of red flags and broken promises, Sterling Homes felt like a genuine partner in the process rather than an adversary.

For the first time in my home-buying journey, I felt confident I had made the right choice.

— Colton English
