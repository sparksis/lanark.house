I want to share some unfiltered feedback about my experience with Sterling Homes. I acknowledge upfront that I've had positive experiences with several members of the team. But the overall post-possession experience has left enough of a bitter taste that I need to lay it all out.

**Mike.**

I've written about Mike separately, but to summarize: as I documented in my post-possession notes, the site supervisor was dismissive and unprofessional. When I raised a concern about the washer and dryer door alignment, Mike dismissed it outright rather than acknowledging the issue. I observed Mike talk over his colleague **Yaz** when she pointed out a visible water stain on the ceiling. As I noted in my post-possession records, Mike provided dangerously wrong information about foam off-gassing on possession day — telling me it would take "half an hour" when the crew said **24 hours and that it was unsafe to stay**. A 3mm piece of cracked plastic on an appliance had been carefully set back in place — which I interpreted as an attempt to hide the defect. Mike shrugged it off.

**The Virtuo app.**

Sterling uses an app called **Virtuo** for warranty support and communication. As a tech-savvy person, I was initially excited about this. The reality was different.

The app had a critical UI bug on my **Google Pixel** phone: the navigation menu rendered *underneath* the native Android navigation buttons, making it physically impossible to tap the "task" icon. This isn't an edge case — Google's Pixel phones have a significant market share. It made core functionality unusable.

Beyond the bug, the "Virtuo agents" on the other end simply never actioned my requests. Days, and then weeks, would go by with no response, no acknowledgement, and no progress. Support inquiries were ignored, tickets were left unresolved for extended periods.

**The eavestrough.**

My initial concerns about the eavestrough were raised on **June 30, 2025**, when I reported improperly sloped gutters leading to persistent pooling of water on the southwest corner.

On **July 11**, I expressed my frustration to **Jaspreet Gill**, noting a **90-degree joint** that did not appear to be properly cut and jointed, and the potential impact of the poor eavestrough installation with water escaping.

**Don Allary** from **Empire Envelope** — the subcontractor — sent a terse reply: *"We have fix the drainage issue with the eavestrough."* The issue was far from resolved, but that was the extent of the response.

**The outsourcing problem.**

Sterling outsources portions of their customer service to third-party agencies. The level of intimacy and comfort you naturally expect from direct communication with the builder is lost when interactions are facilitated by an impersonal third party. This outsourcing disrupted the flow of communication and seriously impacted the quality of the post-possession experience.

**Credit where it's due.**

Several other Sterling staff members were transparent and forthcoming. Transparency and honesty are core values of mine, and I appreciated those who embodied them. But the negative aspects — what I experienced as Mike's dismissiveness, the dysfunctional Virtuo app, the eavestrough runaround, and the outsourced customer service — seriously overshadowed those positive moments.

I hope this feedback serves as a candid wake-up call for Sterling Homes. They have potential and resources, but unless they address these gaps in customer service and professionalism, they risk alienating more homeowners.

— Colton English
