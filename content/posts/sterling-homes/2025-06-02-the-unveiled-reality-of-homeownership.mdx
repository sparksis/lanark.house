The journey from signing the purchase agreement to holding the keys was remarkably smooth. It was everything that came after that tested me.

**Mortgage: May 21, 2025.**

I was working with two lenders, ATB and CIBC, to ensure I was getting the best possible terms. **Jason Tang** was a model of efficiency — he was in constant communication with my mortgage advisor at CIBC, **Heather Hannah**. On **May 21, 2025**, I received the official approval letter from CIBC. After the contract chaos with Douglas Homes and the specification disputes with Jayman, having the financing locked down felt like crossing a real finish line.

**Home inspection: May 28, 2025.**

I hired **Marty Keough** from **Nook & Cranny Home Inspections**, a firm with a reputation for thoroughness. The inspection of **728 Langley Terrace**, conducted on **May 28, 2025**, came back remarkably clean. The list of deficiencies was short and consisted of minor, cosmetic issues that were easily addressed. There were no major structural problems, no plumbing or electrical issues, and no signs of shoddy workmanship.

**Possession day: June 2, 2025.**

June 2, 2025 was possession day. I met **Mike**, the site supervisor, for the walkthrough. At first glance, Mike seemed competent and helpful. The walkthrough itself went smoothly enough. But this was the calm before the storm.

**The first cracks.**

On possession day itself, two incidents set the tone for what was to come.

First, I noticed that the doors on the stacking washer and dryer were hinged on opposite sides, making it awkward to move clothes from one to the other. When I pointed this out and asked if it could be fixed, as I noted in my post-possession records, Mike's response was to tell me I was wrong for thinking the doors should align. It was an unnecessarily defensive reaction to a simple observation.

Second — and more seriously — one of the outstanding items on the possession list was the foam insulation for the basement party wall. As I noted in my post-possession records, Mike assured me this would be quick: "It'll only take about half an hour." When the crew arrived, they told me the foam would **off-gas for approximately 24 hours** and that it would be **unsafe to stay in the house during that time**. On possession day. The day I was supposed to move in. I consider Mike's "half an hour" estimate not just wrong — but dangerously misleading.

**What's ahead.**

The sales process with Sterling was the best of my three builder experiences. Jason Tang, Heather Hannah, and Marty Keough all did their jobs with professionalism and competence. But the post-possession phase — the warranty claims, the Virtuo app, the ongoing repairs, and Mike — would be a different story entirely.

Becoming a homeowner is a milestone and a journey. The path from contract to keys was smooth. The path from keys to "this actually feels like home" is the harder one.

— Colton English
