I took possession of my Sterling Homes property at **728 Langley Terrace SE, Airdrie, AB** on **June 2, 2025**. My one-year builder's warranty expires on **June 2, 2026**. This post is a comprehensive accounting of every warranty and repair item I've dealt with — the good, the bad, and the still-outstanding.

## Warranty Items — Chronological

### Eavestrough (June 30, 2025 → July 11 → ongoing)
Reported improperly sloped eavestroughs leading to persistent pooling of water on the southwest corner on **June 30**. On **July 11**, I flagged a **90-degree joint** that was not properly cut and jointed to **Jaspreet Gill**. **Don Allary** from **Empire Envelope** claimed the drainage issue was fixed. It wasn't. This item remained open and I later sent an email titled "Urgent: Unresolved Eavestrough Deficiencies."

### Stairwell Window (July 15, 2025)
The stairwell window glass was cracked. **Aly Derwent** confirmed the window was scheduled for replacement on **July 15, 2025**.

### Driveway and Walkway (July 24, 2025)
**July 24, 2025** marked a significant milestone: the concrete for the driveway and walkway was finally poured. A Virtuo email from July 15 had noted seasonal delays due to heavy rain. Drive/walkway final grading was also tracked as a separate item.

### Fireplace — Missing Crystals and Not Turning On (August 7, 2025)
The fireplace was missing its decorative crystals and wouldn't turn on. Aly Derwent confirmed they were waiting on a part for the fireplace repair. An appointment with **Enercare** was scheduled for **August 7, 2025**.

### Ceiling Texture Repair — Kitchen (August 18–21, 2025)
A ceiling texture repair was needed in the kitchen area, anticipated to be scheduled between **August 18 and August 21, 2025**. This item was also noted on the inspection sheet (`158004 - First inspection - 728 Langley Terrace.xlsx`).

### Dryer Door (Possession Day)
The dryer door was reversed. Mike was belligerent about it, insisting it was supposed to be that way. Tracked in the Notion database.

### Foam Basement Party Wall (Possession Day)
Foam insulation for the basement party wall was delayed until possession day. Mike claimed it would take 30 minutes. The crew said 24 hours of off-gassing, unsafe to stay. A safety incident on day one.

## Hot Water Failure (December 2025 → ongoing)

In **late December 2025**, the hot water failed. A vendor (Lynx Plumbing) reached out in late January/early February 2026.

**Brendan McElroy** noted: "In order for us to review the issue, we may need to cut some drywall to inspect the water lines and insulation. The water lines are covered with the 2-year distribution warranty."

**Adam Pocock** confirmed: "Lynx Plumbing will attend your upcoming year-end inspection to locate and address the cause of the problem."

## Year-End Inspection

On **April 1, 2026**, **Deborah Curran** emailed: "Your one-year builder's warranty with Sterling Homes concludes on June 2nd." She offered a year-end inspection: **"Would May 26th @ 9:00am work for you for Nick to come over?"**

The year-end inspection on **May 26, 2026** will include **Nick Mintchev** and the Lynx Plumbing investigation into the hot water issue. The warranty expires on **June 2, 2026**.

## The Big Picture

The initial purchase was smooth — much of this was down to Sterling's excellent communication on financing, concierge services, and possession documents. The construction quality was generally good, and the seasonal work (driveway, grading, landscaping) has improved the curb appeal significantly.

The warranty phase, though, has been a test of patience. Between the Virtuo app's dysfunction, the eavestrough runaround, the fireplace delay, and the hot water failure, staying on top of every item has been a part-time job. Keep records. Document everything. And don't let survey fatigue stop you from flagging issues.

Here's to making houses into homes.
