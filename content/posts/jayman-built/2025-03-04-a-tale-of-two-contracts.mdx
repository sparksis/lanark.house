After my experience with Douglas Homes collapsed in November 2024, I started looking again. My second attempt at buying a new home was with Jayman Built, and it began with genuine optimism.

**March 4, 2025: The purchase agreement.**

On March 4, 2025, I received the purchase agreement for a home at **71 Legacy Reach Court SE, Calgary, Alberta**, in the community of **Legacy**. The model was an **Avid 20 with revised ensuite** — not the "Cooper" that some of their marketing referenced, but the Avid 20 specifically. My sales associate was **Brittney Mack**.

The agreement included two key line items:
- **$7,820** for the "Revised Ensuite" — creating a 4-piece ensuite with a separate soaker tub and shower
- **$12,600** for "Fit and Finish Extra (2)"

The contract also referenced specifications from the Jayman Built [Fit and Finish Brochure](https://www.jayman.com/assets/Uploads/JAY-2609-Fit-and-Finish-Brochure-CAL_WEB_2025.pdf), which described the ensuite in specific, enticing terms:

> "4-piece ensuites include curbless tiled shower recessed into the floor sloped towards a 32" linear drain. Walls are tiled to ceiling. Includes 80" height, chrome frame, 10 mil glass shower door."

This brochure wasn't a vague marketing flyer — it read like a specification sheet. The shower it described was a significant part of why I chose this home. The floor plan showed a marked 42" by 48" ensuite shower in the 4-piece configuration.

**The gap between the brochure and the contract.**

When I read the contract closely, the ensuite shower specification was vague. The luxurious details from the brochure — the 80" height, the 10mil glass, the chrome frame — were nowhere in the written agreement. The contract's only reference to the shower was a clause about "other shower stalls applicable to plan," taken from Addendum B, page 4, point 3. No dimensions. No materials. No specifics.

I raised this with Brittney, hoping it was a simple oversight. It wasn't.

**Mid-April: The specs start shrinking.**

On April 10, 2025, Brittney emailed me with what she said the actual specification would be: a **70" height with 5mil glass**. When I challenged this — pointing to the brochure's explicit 80"/10mil promise — the spec was revised on April 11 to **75" height with 6mil glass**. Still not what the brochure promised. And none of these numbers appeared anywhere in the signed contract or its addenda.

Meanwhile, Jayman insisted that upgrading to the 80" height and 10mil glass promised in the brochure was a nearly **$7,000 CAD upgrade**. The brochure had described it as a standard feature. The contract was silent on it. And now they wanted $7,000 to deliver what was advertised.

**The silence.**

On April 11, I requested escalation to the sales manager, **Michael Klassen**. Despite multiple follow-up emails on April 16, April 17, and April 26, no contact was initiated by Mr. Klassen. It took a direct email from me on **April 27** — explicitly stating concern over the good faith nature of the 16-day delay — to finally elicit a response later that same day.

On **April 28, 2025**, I sent a formal dispute letter to **Stephanie Myers**, Vice President of Jayman Built Single Family in Calgary, with copies to **David Hooge** (COO) and **Jay Westman** (Chairman & CEO). The letter laid out the discrepancy, cited the brochure specifications verbatim, and requested remediation including confirmation of a **Navien NPE 240A tankless water heater** as specified in Addendum B.

Under the principle of *contra proferentem* — that ambiguous contract terms should be interpreted against the party that drafted them — I believed my interpretation was reasonable and legally defensible.

**The end.**

In early May, Jayman Built terminated the purchase agreement. The home I had planned at Legacy Reach Court was gone.

This experience taught me that a brochure is not a contract, and that when a builder leaves the contract intentionally vague, the gap between what's advertised and what's delivered can be enormous. If a specification matters to you, demand it in writing — in the contract itself, not in a glossy PDF.

— Colton English
