My experience with Jayman Built came down to a shower — and the silence that followed when I asked them to deliver what they advertised.

I chose Jayman Built for a home at **71 Legacy Reach Court SE** in Calgary's **Legacy** community. The model was an **Avid 20 with revised ensuite**. The purchase agreement arrived on **March 4, 2025**. I paid **$7,820** for the "Revised Ensuite" creating a 4-piece ensuite, and **$12,600** for "Fit and Finish Extra (2)."

Among the features that drew me in, one stood out: the ensuite shower described in Jayman's [Fit and Finish Brochure](https://www.jayman.com/assets/Uploads/JAY-2609-Fit-and-Finish-Brochure-CAL_WEB_2025.pdf):

> "4-piece ensuites include curbless tiled shower recessed into the floor sloped towards a 32" linear drain. Walls are tiled to ceiling. Includes 80" height, chrome frame, 10 mil glass shower door."

This wasn't a vague marketing promise. It read like a specification. The floor plan showed a 42" by 48" shower base. This was a significant part of why I chose this home.

**The contract was silent.**

When the contract landed, that specification was nowhere. The only reference to the shower was Addendum B, page 4, point 3: "other shower stalls applicable to plan." No dimensions, no materials, no glass thickness. The brochure was referenced as "the brochure" without disclaimers. The ambiguity was a glaring red flag.

**The specs kept shrinking.**

I reached out to my sales associate, **Brittney Mack**, to clarify. On **April 10**, Brittney emailed that the specification would be **70" height with 5mil glass**. When I challenged this against the brochure's explicit 80"/10mil, she revised it on **April 11** to **75" height with 6mil glass**. Still not what was advertised. And none of these specs were documented in the signed contract or its addenda.

When I cited the brochure, Brittney referenced Addendum B, page 4, point 3 — "other shower stalls applicable to plan" — but failed to point to any specific clause or document defining the dimensions or materials.

Jayman insisted that upgrading to the 80" height and 10mil glass described in their own brochure was a nearly **$7,000 CAD upgrade**. The brochure described it as standard. The contract was silent. And now they wanted $7,000 to deliver what was advertised.

**The 16-day silence.**

On April 11, I requested escalation to the sales manager, **Michael Klassen**. Despite multiple email follow-ups on **April 16, April 17, and April 26**, no contact was initiated. It took a direct email from me to Mr. Klassen on **April 27**, explicitly stating concern over the good faith nature of the **16-day delay**, to finally elicit a response later that same day.

**The formal dispute.**

On **April 28, 2025**, I sent a formal dispute letter to **Stephanie Myers**, Vice President of Jayman Built Single Family in Calgary, with copies to **David Hooge** (COO) and **Jay Westman** (Chairman & CEO).

The letter requested specific remediations including:
- Shower dimensions: 42" x 48" base, 80" height from threshold, 10mil clear glass, chrome frame, 8" D handle
- Written confirmation, via contract amendment, that the home would include the **Navien NPE 240A tankless water heater** as specified in Addendum B, Page 4

I cited the principle of ***contra proferentem*** — that ambiguous contract terms should be interpreted against the party that drafted them. My interpretation was reasonable and legally defensible.

**The termination.**

In early May, Jayman Built terminated the purchase agreement. The home at Legacy Reach Court was gone.

This story is not just about a shower. It's about the gap between what's advertised and what's delivered, and the silence that fills that gap when you ask for accountability. Jayman's brochure was the bait. The vague contract was the trap. And when I pushed back, the response was weeks of silence followed by termination.

To anyone considering Jayman Built: look beyond the brochure. Get every specification that matters to you written into the contract itself. Watch for vague language. And if you encounter silence when seeking answers, take it as the answer it is.

— Colton English
