As my one-year builder’s warranty with Sterling Homes approaches its expiration on June 2nd, 2026, the pattern of communication from their local office has shifted from simple incompetence to deliberate apathy and systemic negligence. This post documents the total time to completion for resolved items and the ongoing status of those left to rot.

This chronicle serves as a public record and a foundation for formal escalation to Sterling Homes leadership and the Alberta New Home Warranty Program.

### The Saga of Remediation

#### 1. Fireplace Unit Replacement (4+ Months to Resolution)
*   **Initial Report:** June 2025 (at possession)
*   **The Saga:** Multiple service visits by Classic Fireplace involved disassembling and reassembling a non-functional unit. By August 15, 2025, a formal demand for root cause analysis was issued.
*   **Resolution:** A full unit replacement was finally approved by Jaspreet Gill on September 12, 2025, and completed in **October 2025**.
*   **Lessons Learned:** Sterling only acts when the "repair" loop is proven to be a waste of time. It took over four months to get a working fireplace in a brand-new home.
