As my first year at 728 Langley Terrace comes to a close, a significant unresolved issue looms: the complete failure of the hot water delivery system during a severe cold snap in December 2025. This wasn't just an inconvenience; it was a fundamental failure of a critical home system, and the subsequent response from Sterling Homes and its vendors has been illustrative of the "cloudy" processes I've encountered before.

### The December Crisis

The failure occurred in late December, right in the middle of a winter cold snap. I was forced to wait for another cold spell to properly investigate the issue as an emergency call. Lynx Plumbing, the vendor, reached out in late January or early February 2026, but after I left a detailed voicemail for them, they simply never called back.

### Seeking Accountability

On March 19, 2026, I followed up with the Sterling Warranty team to express my frustration. Adam Pocock, my Customer Relations Coordinator, confirmed on March 20th that Lynx Plumbing will attend the upcoming year-end inspection to locate the cause. He also provided a small measure of reassurance: the plumbing (water delivery system) is covered under a 2-year warranty, meaning it will remain eligible for coverage through the following year.

However, the investigation itself is a point of concern. Adam mentioned the possibility of "destructive investigation," such as removing drywall around the plumbing stack to check for missing insulation. For me, this is a non-negotiable situation: I require a clear drawing on the building plan of exactly where any cuts or removals will be made before any trade enters my home to perform such work. My past experiences with nine previous water losses have taught me that clear boundaries and documented plans are the only way to build trust and ensure accountability.

### The Year-End Inspection

As of April 1, 2026, Deborah Curran has requested to schedule my Year-End Inspection for **May 26, 2026, at 9:00 AM**. I've asked for an escalation on the water failure details, as I want a clearer understanding of the scope of inspection before Nick, the warranty tech, and Lynx Plumbing arrive.

The countdown to June 2, 2025—the official conclusion of my one-year builder's warranty—is on. This year-end inspection is the final chance to ensure that every outstanding grievance, from the roof cleaning to the eavestrough pooling and the fireplace unit replacement, is addressed.

**Next Steps:** I will prepare a detailed checklist for the May 26th inspection, including the requirement for building plan markups for any plumbing investigation. I will also follow up on the specific escalation I requested to ensure that someone above the coordinator level is aware of my concerns regarding the water failure root cause analysis.
